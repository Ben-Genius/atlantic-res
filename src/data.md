1. Executive Summary
This document presents a comprehensive extraction and analysis of the existing Atlantic Catering & Logistics Limited website (https://atlanticcatering-gh.com/) alongside detailed recommendations for a complete redesign. Atlantic Catering is a wholly-owned Ghanaian professional corporate catering company established in 2014, specializing in onshore and offshore catering operations, camp management, ship and store supplies, and various support services including housekeeping, laundry, and janitorial services. The company holds all three ISO certifications and serves major clients in Ghana's oil and gas industry, including operations aboard two FPSOs.
The current website, built on WordPress with the Salient theme and WPBakery page builder, contains valuable content across seven primary pages and multiple sustainability sub-pages. However, the site suffers from outdated design patterns, limited mobile responsiveness, poor visual hierarchy, and missed opportunities for user engagement. The redesign recommendations in this document address visual design, user experience, content strategy, technical architecture, mobile optimization, and search engine performance to transform the website into a modern, conversion-focused digital platform that properly reflects Atlantic Catering's industry leadership and ISO-certified quality standards.

4. Current Design Analysis
4.1 Visual Design Assessment
The current website employs the Salient theme's Material design variant with a clean white background, green accent color (#4cba48), and dark typography. While the color palette is appropriate for a catering company (green suggesting freshness and sustainability), the execution lacks sophistication. The parallax hero section with background video is a good foundation, but the overall visual treatment feels dated compared to contemporary web design standards. The typography relies primarily on Lato for navigation and body text, with Playfair Display used sparingly, resulting in limited typographic hierarchy and visual interest.
The homepage layout follows a standard corporate pattern: hero banner, two alternating content blocks, news carousel, and footer. This linear structure fails to create visual momentum or guide users toward key conversion points. The service pages use a simple icon-and-text grid layout that does not adequately differentiate the diverse range of offerings or communicate the premium quality that ISO certification implies. Image usage across the site appears sparse, with large text-heavy sections that lack the appetizing food photography and dynamic imagery one would expect from a leading catering company.
4.2 User Experience Issues
Several significant user experience problems have been identified in the current design. The navigation structure, while comprehensive, presents cognitive overload through the deeply nested Sustainability mega-menu containing nine sub-items across three levels. Users must hover and carefully navigate this complex dropdown structure, which is particularly challenging on mobile devices. The eCommerce link redirects to an entirely separate Odoo platform, creating a disjointed experience for users who wish to order services online.
The contact form, while functional, lacks modern features such as real-time validation, file attachment capability for event inquiries, and preferred contact method selection. There is no live chat option, no FAQ section, and no clear escalation path for urgent inquiries. The news section operates as a simple chronological list with no categorization beyond a single Press Release filter, no search functionality, and no related article suggestions. Client testimonials are limited to just two entries, which insufficiently demonstrates the company's track record.
4.3 Mobile Experience
The Salient theme includes responsive design capabilities, but the mobile experience reveals several shortcomings. The mega-menu collapses into a slide-out widget area that becomes difficult to navigate with the Sustainability sub-menu's three-level hierarchy. The parallax hero section, while visually appealing on desktop, can cause performance issues on mobile devices and may not render the video background effectively on slower connections. The news carousel pagination dots are small touch targets, and the overall mobile layout does not prioritize the most important content and calls-to-action for on-the-go users.
4.4 Content Gaps
The website lacks several content elements that are standard for a company of Atlantic Catering's caliber. There is no dedicated careers or recruitment page despite the company employing over 150 staff members and emphasizing local capacity development. No case studies or project portfolios demonstrate the company's successful delivery on major contracts. The service pages provide only brief descriptions without supporting imagery, process explanations, or pricing frameworks. There is no blog or thought leadership section to showcase industry expertise. The company's impressive ISO certifications are mentioned but not detailed or visually presented with certificate imagery.
5. Redesign Suggestions
The following recommendations are organized into six strategic areas that collectively address the current website's shortcomings and position Atlantic Catering for a modern, conversion-optimized digital presence.
5.1 Visual Design & Branding
5.1.1 Color System Refinement
The current green accent (#4cba48) should be evolved into a more sophisticated, premium color system. Consider a deep forest green as the primary brand color (reflecting both freshness and the company's Ghanaian heritage), paired with warm gold accents that convey premium quality and align with the existing corporate identity. The background should shift from pure white to warm off-white tones (#FAFAF8) that create a more inviting, hospitality-appropriate atmosphere. Dark sections should use deep charcoal (#1A2330) rather than pure black for a more luxurious feel.
5.1.2 Typography Overhaul
Replace the current Lato-based system with a carefully curated type pairing that communicates professionalism and warmth. A strong serif typeface such as Playfair Display or Cormorant Garamond for headings creates an upscale hospitality feel, while a clean sans-serif like Inter or DM Sans ensures readability for body text. Implement a clear typographic scale with at least five distinct levels (display, H1, H2, H3, body) to create strong visual hierarchy and guide the reader's eye through content sections.
5.1.3 Photography & Visual Assets
Invest in professional food photography, kitchen operations imagery, and offshore facility documentation. The website should feature a rich visual library including: hero images of prepared dishes with Ghanaian-inspired presentation, behind-the-scenes kitchen operations showing ISO-certified processes, offshore and remote site catering setups, event catering in action, and team members at work. Implement a consistent image treatment with subtle warm color grading and soft shadows to create a cohesive visual identity across all pages.
5.1.4 Motion & Micro-interactions
Introduce subtle animations and micro-interactions that enhance the user experience without compromising performance. Smooth scroll-triggered reveal animations for content sections, hover effects on service cards that provide additional detail, animated counters for key statistics (years in business, meals served, team size), and gentle parallax effects on select hero sections. All animations should respect the prefers-reduced-motion media query for accessibility compliance.
5.2 User Experience & Navigation
5.2.1 Simplified Navigation Architecture
Restructure the navigation to reduce cognitive load while maintaining access to all content. Replace the deep three-level Sustainability mega-menu with a dedicated Sustainability hub page that acts as a gateway to sub-content. The primary navigation should be limited to six items: Home, About Us, Services, Sustainability, News, and Contact. Move the eCommerce link to a prominent header button rather than a navigation item. Consider using a sticky navigation bar that collapses to a minimal version on scroll, with a clear call-to-action button always visible.
5.2.2 Service Pages Redesign
Transform each service page from a simple text block into a comprehensive service landing page. Each service should include: a hero section with relevant imagery, a clear value proposition, a detailed process or workflow section, relevant case studies or project highlights, client testimonials specific to that service, pricing framework or request-a-quote functionality, related services cross-promotion, and a dedicated contact form or inquiry button. This approach transforms passive information pages into active conversion funnels.
5.2.3 Enhanced Contact Experience
Redesign the contact page as a conversion hub with multiple engagement paths. Include: a prominent phone number and email with click-to-call functionality, an intelligent contact form that routes inquiries to the appropriate department based on service selection, a live chat option for immediate assistance, an FAQ section addressing common inquiries, a meeting scheduler integration for consultation bookings, an interactive map showing the office location, and multiple trust signals (ISO badges, client logos, award icons).
5.2.4 Client Showcase Transformation
Expand the Clients page into a compelling social proof section. Develop detailed case studies with quantified results (e.g., meals served per day, client satisfaction scores, contract duration). Create industry-specific client segments (oil and gas, corporate, events) with tailored messaging. Implement a client logo carousel with hover effects showing brief engagement details. Add video testimonials alongside written ones, and include industry recognition and award imagery.
5.3 Content Strategy
5.3.1 Homepage Content Architecture
Redesign the homepage as a strategic narrative journey that guides visitors from awareness to action. The new homepage flow should follow this structure: (1) Immersive hero section with powerful headline, value proposition, and dual CTAs (Learn More and Request a Quote); (2) Trust bar showing key credentials (ISO Certified, 10+ Years, 150+ Team, 99.9% Ghanaian); (3) Services overview with interactive cards linking to detailed pages; (4) Featured case study or project highlight; (5) Client logo wall and testimonial carousel; (6) ESG commitment preview linking to the sustainability hub; (7) Latest news with category filters; (8) Conversion-focused CTA section with contact form or consultation booking.
5.3.2 New Content Pages
Several new pages should be created to address current content gaps and support business objectives. A Careers page should showcase the company culture, list open positions, and highlight the 99.9% Ghanaian workforce commitment. A Case Studies page should present detailed project narratives with challenges, solutions, and measurable outcomes. An About Our Team page should feature key leadership profiles beyond the CEO. A Certifications page should detail all three ISO certifications with certificate imagery and compliance explanations. A Blog or Insights section should establish thought leadership in corporate catering and ESG practices.
5.3.3 Content Tone & Voice
Establish a consistent brand voice that balances professionalism with warmth. The current content swings between corporate formality and casual friendliness. The redesigned content should consistently project: confidence born from expertise (not arrogance), warmth that reflects Ghanaian hospitality values, precision that reflects ISO-certified processes, and approachability that encourages inquiries from prospective clients. All content should be written in active voice with concrete details rather than vague claims.
5.4 Technical Improvements
5.4.1 Platform Migration Consideration
Evaluate migrating from the WPBakery page builder to a modern WordPress block editor (Gutenberg) or a headless CMS approach using Next.js or similar framework. WPBakery generates excessive inline styles and bloated markup that negatively impacts page load performance and makes content updates difficult for non-technical staff. A modern stack would provide: significantly faster page loads through server-side rendering and static generation, a component-based architecture that ensures design consistency, built-in responsive design without theme dependencies, improved content editing workflow for the marketing team, and better integration capabilities with the Odoo eCommerce platform.
5.4.2 Performance Optimization
Implement comprehensive performance optimizations targeting Core Web Vitals benchmarks. Key improvements include: lazy loading for all below-fold images and videos, WebP/AVIF image format serving with fallbacks, critical CSS inlining with deferred non-critical styles, JavaScript bundling and code splitting to reduce initial payload, CDN deployment for static assets with edge caching, font optimization using font-display: swap and preloading, database query optimization for WordPress, and implementation of server-side caching with appropriate cache invalidation strategies.
5.4.3 eCommerce Integration
Replace the current external Odoo subdomain approach with a seamless eCommerce integration. Options include: embedding the Odoo ordering platform within the main site using an iframe with custom styling for visual consistency, building a custom ordering interface using the Odoo API as a backend, or migrating to a WordPress-native eCommerce solution such as WooCommerce with custom product types for catering services. The goal is to eliminate the jarring transition between the main site and the ordering platform, creating a unified brand experience.
5.4.4 Analytics & Conversion Tracking
Implement comprehensive analytics to measure and optimize website performance. Set up Google Analytics 4 with enhanced ecommerce tracking, define and track key conversion events (contact form submissions, quote requests, eCommerce orders, phone clicks), implement heatmap tools (Hotjar or Microsoft Clarity) to understand user behavior, create custom dashboards for marketing team monitoring, and establish A/B testing infrastructure for continuous optimization of key pages and conversion elements.
5.5 Mobile Experience
5.5.1 Mobile-First Design Approach
Adopt a mobile-first design methodology where all layouts and interactions are designed for mobile devices first, then progressively enhanced for larger screens. This reflects the reality of internet usage in Ghana, where mobile devices account for the majority of web traffic. Key mobile-specific considerations include: thumb-friendly navigation with bottom navigation bars, tap-optimized touch targets (minimum 44x44 pixels), streamlined forms with appropriate input types (tel for phone numbers, email for email fields), swipe gestures for image carousels and card layouts, optimized image delivery based on device capabilities and connection speed, and offline capability for key information like contact details and service summaries.
5.5.2 Performance on Constrained Networks
Ghana's internet infrastructure includes a mix of 3G, 4G, and emerging 5G connectivity, with many users on limited data plans. The redesign must prioritize performance on constrained networks through: aggressive image compression and resolution adaptation based on connection speed, minimal JavaScript payloads for initial page render, progressive content loading that shows text before images, service worker caching for repeat visits, data-saving mode that reduces bandwidth consumption, and lightweight alternatives for rich content like video backgrounds (e.g., static images with play-on-demand video).
5.6 SEO & Online Presence
5.6.1 Technical SEO Foundation
Establish a robust technical SEO foundation that ensures search engines can efficiently crawl, index, and understand the website. This includes: implementing structured data markup (Schema.org) for Organization, LocalBusiness, Service, and FAQ types, creating a comprehensive XML sitemap with proper priority and change frequency settings, optimizing meta titles and descriptions for all pages with keyword research, implementing proper heading hierarchy (single H1 per page, logical H2-H6 structure), ensuring all images have descriptive alt text, creating proper URL structure with keyword-rich slugs, and implementing canonical URLs to prevent duplicate content issues.
5.6.2 Local SEO Optimization
As a Ghana-based company serving primarily local and regional clients, local SEO is critical. Key actions include: claiming and optimizing the Google Business Profile with accurate NAP (Name, Address, Phone) information, creating location-specific landing pages for key service areas (Accra, Takoradi, offshore operations), implementing local business schema markup, building citations on Ghanaian business directories, encouraging and managing client reviews on Google and industry platforms, and creating content targeting local search queries (e.g., corporate catering Accra, offshore catering Ghana).
5.6.3 Content Marketing Strategy
Develop a content marketing strategy that positions Atlantic Catering as a thought leader in corporate catering and ESG practices in West Africa. This should include: a regularly updated blog covering topics such as catering trends, ESG best practices, Ghanaian cuisine innovations, and industry insights; downloadable resources such as event planning guides, catering checklists, and sustainability reports; video content showcasing kitchen operations, client testimonials, and behind-the-scenes stories; email newsletter integration to nurture leads and maintain client engagement; and social media integration that automatically shares new content across platforms.
